How it works

A local, zero-dependency tool that turns a brand's real truths into emotionally-honest static ad concepts — grounded, quality-gated, and rendered into finished images. This page walks the user flow, the data flow, and how concepts are actually generated.

02The user flow

What a strategist actually clicks through, start to finish.

  1. Pick a brand. The app loads that brand's grounding deck (its verified facts, personas, and voice). The UI — Brief bar and board — is usable immediately; only generation needs an API key.
  2. Set the Brief. Optionally pin a persona, pain/desire, awareness stage, format, CTA, etc. Anything left blank is chosen per-concept. Nothing pinned = maximum spread.
  3. 🔍 Mine insights. AI Pick a persona and the app imagines her inner life, surfacing ~8 raw human tensions she'd never say aloud (envy, shame, grief, fear). You select the truest 3–4 as the emotional core.
  4. Generate. AI The engine produces a hand of concept cards — each a fundamentally different ad type, built on a real product truth and a human tension. A hidden judge silently discards anything weak; you only see cards that passed.
  5. Curate on the board. Keep the ones you love (they can seed variations), discard the rest. Comment on any line to regenerate it.
  6. Finalize a concept. AI Promote a card to a polished spec: headline, tagline variants, on-image copy, a "why it works," and a compliance check.
  7. Build the ad from a template. AI The app auto-matches a proven ad-layout template (from a library of 300), narrowed to the best matches for this concept. Swap it for any other if you like.
  8. 🎨 Generate the image. AI Nano Banana Pro renders the finished ad in-app (1K/2K/4K), using your product photo as reference. Download, regenerate, or export the record.

03The data flow

Where the facts come from and how they're kept honest. The golden rule: concepts may only use facts from the grounding deck — nothing is invented.

Building the grounding deck

A hand-written brand-context/<brand>.md is distilled once into a compact, structured <brand>.deck.json (cached on disk).

Personas carry not just name / life / wants but an inner-life layer — inner monologue, unspoken fears, who she envies, the moments that sting, who she was vs. wants to be. Legacy decks are enriched automatically on load. Sonnet

Pains carry the functional problem plus real voice-of-customer phrases. Plus product truths, proof points, brand voice, and banned language.

How the deck is used

The deck renders into one compact text block that is injected — with prompt-caching — into both the generator and the judge, so both share the exact same notion of "true" and "on-brand."

Selected insights and Brief pins live on the session. Mined-but-unused insights are cached so re-opening the tray never re-mines.

Nothing outside the deck counts as fact. The judge treats any stat or number not in the proof points as a fabrication and fails it.

04How concepts are generated

The heart of the tool. Three ideas work together to make concepts that are grounded, emotionally true, and genuinely varied.

① Emotional core

Every concept is built on a real human tension — the thing she feels but won't say — surfaced with empathy, never mockery. This is what lifts an ad from a functional pain ("dry skin") to a truth ("my arms give my age away before I do").

② Diversity engine

Before generating, each card in the batch is assigned a distinct awareness × mechanic × hook-tactic × format (and one insight, rotated). So a hand is a Confession UGC selfie and a stat card and a before/after — not one line reworded. The overused "X isn't Y, it's Z" reframe is capped at one card.

③ Quality gate

A skeptical judge scores every candidate. The engine over-generates and silently discards anything below the bar or non-compliant, so you only ever see concepts that passed. Weak cards never reach the board.

What the judge rewards

Six axes, weighted. Emotional truth now sits alongside product truth — a raw, empathetic human truth scores high; a generic platitude scores low. Compliance is a separate hard gate.

AxisWeightWhat it measures
Product truth 24%Anchored in a real brand mechanism / proof — not a generic claim.
Emotional truth 22%A real, uncomfortable human truth, surfaced with empathy — not feel-good filler.
Specificity 22%Unmistakably for THIS persona about THIS pain.
Concreteness 13%Vivid, specific detail — not vague platitude.
Scroll-stop 12%Earns the stop for its awareness stage.
Brand voice 7%On-tone; zero banned language.
Hard compliance gate. Any banned term, medical/disease claim, or brand-constraint violation fails instantly, whatever the score. Exploring a raw emotion is not a violation — only banned claims or shaming the viewer are. Pass bar: overall ≥ 70.

05From concept to finished image

Composition isn't freestyled — that produced pretty photos but weak ads. Instead a proven ad-layout template is filled with the concept's copy.

Template match

300 real-ad layout templates (each a prompt with [PLACEHOLDER] tokens) are ranked for the concept by category + tagline keywords; the picker shows the ~15 best matches with a "show all" toggle. instant

Fill & render

Opus fills the template's tokens from the concept copy + brand deck — compliance-safe, never inventing stats. Opus Then Nano Banana Pro renders the finished ad from that prompt + your product reference photo. Nano Banana Pro
